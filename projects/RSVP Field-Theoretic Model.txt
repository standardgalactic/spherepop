Shuffling gene expression only within arbitrarily separated clusters of neurons introduced artificial gaps, showing that the original data do not naturally separate into discrete subtypes. Thus, the transcriptomic heterogeneity across LPLC2 neurons forms a continuous gradient.

The distribution of neurons along PC1 also did not correlate with developmental age, sex, genetic background or mRNA coverage (Fig. 1l and Extended Data Figs. 2e and 3a,b), indicating that molecular heterogeneity had a different origin. The graded expression of the top differentially expressed genes associated with PC1 at 48â€‰h APF (dpr13, beat-VI and tei (encoding IgSF molecules), as well as SiaT (encoding sialyltransferase)) persisted through development (Fig. 1m).

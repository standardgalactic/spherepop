## The Myth of the Clean Slate: Reversibility vs Reality

Modern computing treats state as something that can be updated, overwritten, or reversed at will. The “Undo” button represents this worldview: the idea that the path taken does not matter, only the final result.

Spherepop rejects this assumption.

In the physical world, time is irreversible. Actions leave residue. Identity is not a snapshot—it is a history.

Spherepop proposes:

> A system is not what it is now.  
> A system is how it got there.

## The Mechanics of Commitment

Spherepop is governed by two evolving structures:

- Option Space: Ω_t  
- Commitment History: H_t  

### The Bubble Analogy

Each possible future is a bubble.

To act is to pop one.

That bubble disappears forever and is recorded in history.

- Cannot be undone

## The Lagrangian of Choice

Each action carries a cost.

Define:

    L_t = ΔΩ_t + λΔC_t

- ΔΩ_t → loss of future options  
- ΔC_t → normative or ethical weight  

### Commitment Momentum

As options are eliminated:

- Freedom decreases  
- Commitment increases  

A system that never commits remains pure potential—but never becomes real.

## The Ethics of Refusal

Spherepop introduces a fourth operator: **Refuse**

- Removes an option  
- Records intention  

### Distinction

- Pop → “I didn’t choose this”  
- Refuse → “I will not choose this”  

Refusal creates structure.

It defines boundaries.  
It stabilizes identity.

# Systems of Legibility and Causal Sovereignty

This folder contains a coordinated set of essays and technical specifications examining the structural crisis of modern digital platforms and outlining a constitutional alternative based on causal sovereignty, portable history, and systemic legibility.

Together, these documents analyze how contemporary platforms have evolved from human-centered social spaces into extractive behavioral marketplaces. They argue that institutional power today is exercised through a monopoly over temporal sequence: by controlling the authoritative record of events, platforms govern digital reality itself. This produces structural hostility in the interface, cognitive fragmentation through advertising saturation, and civilizational instability through material and informational leakage.

In response, the texts propose Spherepop: a history-first architecture in which causality becomes a public object, digital histories are mechanically portable, and exit is a routine right rather than a social death. This framework unifies digital sovereignty with material legibility, aiming to restore individual agency and enable long-term civilizational closure.

## Unifying Thesis

These texts collectively argue that:

* Digital decay is structural, not accidental.
* Platform power is rooted in control of time, not merely data.
* Cognitive fragmentation and material waste are expressions of the same thermodynamic failure.
* History must become portable, verifiable, and sovereign.
* Legibility across information and matter is the foundation of a sustainable civilization.

Spherepop is presented not as a product, but as a constitutional architecture for reality itself — one in which individuals carry their past intact, systems compete on service rather than captivity, and both digital and physical worlds are governed by transparency rather than extraction.

This collection defines a transition point: from institutional control of reality to participant ownership of causality.

Extension Axioms

This page records conservative extensions to the core axioms of Spherepop OS. Each extension preserves determinism, replayability, and semantic primacy.

No extension is required for the core system to remain well-defined.

Principle of Conservative Extension

Axiom X0 (Conservativity)

Any extension to Spherepop OS must preserve all core axioms. In particular, no extension may introduce hidden state, causal ambiguity, or alternative sources of authority.

Extensions may add structure, not power.

Distributed Arbitration

Axiom D1 (Logical Single Sequencer)

Even in a distributed deployment, there exists a logical single sequencer whose output defines the authoritative total order of events.

Physical implementation may be replicated or fault-tolerant, but the observable log order must be indistinguishable from a single source.

Theorem D1.1

Distributed arbitration is observationally equivalent to a single arbiter provided that the emitted event sequence is totally ordered and immutable.

Corollary D1.2

Consensus protocols may be used internally without altering kernel semantics.

Entropy-Aware Scheduling

Axiom EΣ1 (Entropy as Metadata)

Measures of entropy, cost, or uncertainty may be attached to events or objects as metadata, provided they do not influence kernel transitions.

Axiom EΣ2 (Advisory Influence Only)

Entropy annotations may guide scheduling, visualization, or prioritization, but may not alter the meaning or order of committed events.

Theorem EΣ2.1

Entropy-aware scheduling preserves deterministic replay so long as entropy values are treated as non-causal annotations.

Typed Semantic Relations

Axiom TΣ1 (Type Enrichment)

Relation types may be extended with richer internal structure (e.g. arity, constraints, annotations), provided normalization remains representative-based.

Axiom TΣ2 (Type Erasure for Replay)

The kernel must admit a type-erased interpretation sufficient to replay all events deterministically.

Theorem TΣ2.1

Type enrichment does not affect replay equivalence if erasure preserves event identity and order.

Higher-Order Events

Axiom H1 (Events About Events)

Events may reference other events (e.g. annotations, justification, provenance) provided such references do not alter prior semantics.

Axiom H2 (Non-Retroactivity)

No event may retroactively modify the semantic effect of a previously committed event.

Theorem H2.1

Provenance, explanation, and commentary layers may be added without violating determinism.

Semantic Compression and Summarization

Axiom C1 (Derived Summaries)

Summaries, compressions, or abstractions of event history are admissible only as derived views.

Axiom C2 (Loss Transparency)

Any lossy summary must declare its loss model explicitly and remain traceable to the authoritative log.

Theorem C2.1

Semantic compression does not compromise correctness if the authoritative log remains available for inspection.

Interoperation with External Systems

Axiom I1 (Boundary Explicitness)

Any interaction with external systems must cross an explicit boundary that converts side effects into proposed events.

Axiom I2 (No Hidden Authority)

External systems may not be treated as authoritative sources of semantic truth.

Theorem I2.1

Side-effectful environments may be safely interfaced with Spherepop OS by treating effects as observations or proposals.

Summary of Extension Discipline

Spherepop OS grows by accretion, not mutation. Extensions add structure while preserving the invariants that make deterministic collaboration possible.

Any system that violates these extension axioms is no longer a Spherepop system.
